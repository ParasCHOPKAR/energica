DISTRESSED ASSETS

A feasibility study for reconstructing distressed renewable energy assets assesses market conditions, technical and financial status, reconstruction costs, and revenue projections. It reviews funding options, calculates ROI, and identifies risks. The study culminates in a strategic plan with recommendations, ensuring the financial and technical viability of asset revitalization

INFRA DEVELOPMENT

Infrastructure development for renewable energy projects involves site selection, design, planning, and financing. It includes securing permits, constructing infrastructure, and installing technologies. Post-installation, systems are tested and commissioned. Ongoing maintenance, performance monitoring, community engagement, and environmental management are crucial for project success and sustainability

LAND PROCUREMENT

For KUSUM and private CAPEX open access renewable projects, selecting land near substations reduces transmission costs and simplifies grid integration. Ensure substations can handle extra capacity and comply with regulations. This choice balances land costs with infrastructure savings. Conduct environmental impact assessments and engage stakeholders for approval and support, aligning with project goals
