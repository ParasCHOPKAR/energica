PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana

The "PM-Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana" (Free Electricity Scheme) is an Indian government initiative launched on February 15, 2024, to provide free electricity to households by installing rooftop solar panels. The scheme aims to help 1 crore households generate their own electricity, with a target of providing up to 300 units of free electricity each month to beneficiaries. It includes installing solar rooftop systems, offering subsidies, and providing low-interest loans to eligible households.

KUSUM Scheme

KUSUM is the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan, a scheme launched in March 2019 to provide solar irrigation pumps to farmers to reduce their costs and increase income. The scheme aims to de-dieselize the agricultural sector by installing solar pumps, offering subsidies on their purchase and other solar projects on barren land.

View More

SMART Program

The SMART (Swayampurna Maharashtra Residential Rooftop) Solar Scheme's objectives are to empower low-income households (BPL/under 100 units/month) with self-reliant solar power to cut bills, boost state renewable energy capacity, reduce grid load, align with national climate goals (Net Zero 2070), and foster local "Made in India" solar manufacturing, supporting energy equity and sustainability through significant state subsidies.

View More

Green Open Access

Maharashtra's Green Energy Open Access (GEOA) Scheme allows consumers (100 kW load or more) to buy renewable power directly from developers, bypassing DISCOMs for cheaper, cleaner energy, governed by MERC rules with simplified processes and metering for HT/LT users, reducing costs and emissions for C&I sectors. Key features include load threshold reduction, special meters (HT/ToD for LT), web portal application via https://greenopenaccess.in/, and exemptions for certain waste/hydrogen projects, aiming to boost solar adoption.
